4/10/24

Somehow books on money have popped up in my life. I’m embarrassed to write this but I read Ramit Sethi’s ‘I Will Teach You To Be Rich.’ I saw it in the library catalog and it looked like something I should read, so I did.

This is a breakdown of his spending plan:

Fixed costs are 50-60% of take home pay. This is rent, utilities, transportation, groceries, internet.
Investments are 10%.
Savings goals are 5-10%. Vacations, gifts, house down payments (lmao), emergency funds.
Lifestyle and spending are 20-35%. Restaurants, clothes, a gym membership, yoga classes, groceries, race money, concert tickets etc.

I wanted to find out my expense breakdown currently. Wells Fargo only gives you a csv of the past 120 days of your purchases, which means this is data from December 21st 2023 to April 7th 2024. It’s not exactly accurate because I’m in a really privileged position regarding finances at the moment, but I'll still look at what I ordinarily spend money on. And how much.
