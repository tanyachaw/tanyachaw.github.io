June 2021

As I wrapped up my first virtual year of college, I wanted to get back into reading. I had heard of Katherine Boo through a podcast earlier in the year, and knew she wrote a book about India. Since my plans to travel to India fell through, a book about a slum in Mumbai seemed like a decent substitute.
I’m not sure if my travels to Mumbai or experience with seeing poverty in New Delhi were confounding factors in how vivid this book was for me, but this might have been the most visual book I’ve read. Rather than giving the reader a background on the political economy of India, Boo drops them straight in Annawadi. She made a 4-year journalistic venture read like a novel, and still managed to capture her voice with the succinct writing.
My time in New Delhi trained me to dehumanize the collectors, scavengers, rickshaw drivers, and other migrant workers. While I denied the existence of caste-based discrimination in modern India, I forgot that my grandmother had a seperate set of utensils for our maid. This book brought those contradictions back to me.
