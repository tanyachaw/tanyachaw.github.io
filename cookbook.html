1. Palak Chicken
Simple, high protein, high in iron.

In a pot of simmering water, add a bundle of spinach, a piece of ginger, 2 garlic cloves, a small tomato, and salt. Put a lid on it until the spinach is wilted. Then strain everything, cool it (I put ice on it), and blend it.

Take an empty pot, add oil, wait for it to heat up, and add jeera (cumin). After it crackles, add the spinach blend and garam masala. Boil for five minutes with the lid. Then add around 200g of cut marinated chicken (or whatever chicken you have). Boil for two minutes with the lid.

2. Jeera Biscuits

1/2 cup butter
1 and 1/2 cups wheat flour (atta)
1/2 tsp salt
1/2 tsp baking powder
1/4 tsp baking soda
1/3 cup sugar
1 tbsp jeera
4-6 tbsp milk

Whisk softened butter and sugar until it's smooth. Sieve in wheat flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda, and mix until it's crumbly. Add in the milk and knead the dough (it should be soft). Dust a surface and roll out the dough. With a cookie cutter or a mixing jigger, cut out circles. Preheat the oven to 355 degrees F. Put each cookie on parchment paper and press in some jeera on top. Bake for 15-17 minutes.

3. Overnight proats
32g protein.

1/2 cup oats
1 tbsp chia seeds
1/2 tsp cinnamon
1/3 cup frozen berries
1 scoop unflavored whey protein
1 tbsp pumpkin seeds/flax seeds/sunflower seeds (optional)

Add oats, chia seeds, cinnamon, frozen berries, and water to some type of storage container before you sleep. When you wake up, mix in the whey protein and adjust water if needed. Add roasted pumpkin, flax, and/or sunflower seeds.
