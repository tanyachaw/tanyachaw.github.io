The Interview

1/3/25

"Most men and women lead lives, if not of quiet desperation, at least of desperate quietness, and they jump at a chance to talk about their work to an outsider who seems eager to listen."

My first interview was god awful. I kept wanting to jump in with my next question on the list without listening to the answer of the previous one. Luckily you relax as you keep interviewing, because ultimately the thing you need is acceptance, genuine interest, and some good listening skills.

We can’t change people, but we want to. We want to mend them to suit us. A nice part of interviewing or profiling someone is that it’s in the job to accept them as they are, to learn who they are. There’s no incentive to form a relationship with them, so there’s no incentive to judge them. It's a discovery. And that’s the thing. In all our relationships, we have a hard time accepting people as they are, with their quirks and oddities. But in an interview, those oddities make this person who they are, and we appreciate them.

Acceptance One way to accept other people is to accept yourself. Start to view yourself objectively, as opposed to personalizing everything. Then it gets easier to do the same with other people.

Genuine interest There’s this thing about “good talkers,” people who just sound so good on tape because of their way of talking. There’s something so unabashedly authentic about them that it leaks on the phone. Or maybe they’re talking about something that really gets them riled up. I once spoke to a philosophy major turned Michelin star chef who had a lot to say about life. It’s those people you’re looking for.

“Most men and women lead lives, if not of quiet desperation, at least of desperate quietness, and they jump at a chance to talk about their work to an outsider who seems eager to listen.”
