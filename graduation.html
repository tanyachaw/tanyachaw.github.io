Graduation (a photo essay)

5/11/24

After four years of sheer trauma and tumult, I graduated from UC Berkeley. I feel like I just went through war. I took FIVE final exams last week. FIVE. FIVE. And the last one was from 7pm to 10pm on a class I DIDN'T EVEN TAKE THIS SEMESTER. And now I have a deep desire to be submerged in a body of water for a bit.

When I got into this school, I cried. Throughout my four years here, I also cried. Because of frustration, insecurities, happiness, hormone fluctuations, and yoga. Several professors touched my life deeply. I told some of them, I didn’t tell others.

I made friends, lost friends, made acquaintances, lost acquaintances, lost myself for a bit too, found myself again - or not, who the fuck knows.

During covid, I saw an article that said this school will beat the shit out of you but you’ll come out stronger. I think that’s largely true and applies to anyone who went here. At least those who majored in something STEM and largely questioned their will to live throughout their time here. We can all trauma bond.

I guess it wasn’t all bad. So here are some things I think I’ll miss.
